    Proteins are essential building blocks of biological systems, functioning through sequence-structure-function relationships. Made up of several components, potential protein-protein interactions (PPIs) are vast in living organisms. The discovery of such knowledge experimentally or computationally can be costly yet inefficient. This research project aims to leverage available protein data and current machine learning techniques to predict potential protein-protein interactions. In utilizing 1D modalities of protein sequences, we aim at comparing the different sequence repre- sentations and their neural network embeddings to predict whether any two proteins interact as well as to compare the varying accuracies of various embedding networks.
